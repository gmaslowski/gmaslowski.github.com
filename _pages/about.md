<p class="about-lead">I build the software that keeps electric fleets moving — and I care just as much about the architecture underneath as the buses on the road.</p>

I'm Greg. By day I'm CTO at [Tenix](https://tenix.eu), where we make charge management software for electric bus fleets. By instinct I'm a software architect who still reads the code, and by genuine enthusiasm I'm the person who gets excited about depot charging windows and OCPP edge cases at dinner.

My work lives where two worlds meet: **software engineering** and **fleet electrification**. Most of the interesting problems — and most of the value — show up in the gap between them.

## How I think about it

Good technology isn't clever for its own sake. The best decision is sometimes a sophisticated system, sometimes deleting code, sometimes saying no. The job is knowing the difference — and being able to explain it to an engineer, a driver, and a CFO in the same afternoon.

Fifteen years in, the questions changed from *"how do we build this?"* to *"should we, what will it unlock, and who maintains it when it breaks?"* I still read code, because code tells the truth. So do incidents, support tickets, and a depot that has to dispatch on time tomorrow morning.
